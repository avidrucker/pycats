# Mechanics FAQ

A plain, question-first Q&A about how pycats works and where it does — and doesn't —
match Project M. Each entry is a short answer with a source link. For the deeper,
fully-sourced write-ups behind these answers, see [`docs/pm-reference/`](./pm-reference/).

## Can you hold a fully-charged smash attack?

**No — not in pycats, and not in Project M.** A smash auto-releases the moment it
reaches full charge. **Holding** a fully-charged smash — staying charged and firing on
release — is an **Ultimate-exclusive** feature; Melee, Brawl, SSB4, and Project M all
auto-release at the charge cap.

**Sources:** SmashWiki — [Smash attack](https://www.ssbwiki.com/Smash_attack) ("Melee,
Brawl, and SSB4 all use the standard 60-frame charge with no hold mechanic — this
feature is exclusive to Ultimate") and [Project M](https://www.ssbwiki.com/Project_M).
